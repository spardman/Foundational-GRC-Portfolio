# Risk Assessment Methodology

## 1. Likelihood Criteria (1-5)

| Level | Descriptor | Description | Frequency |
| :---: | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 5 | Almost Certain | Extremely likely to occur; documented history. | Monthly |
| 4 | Likely | Probable; expected to occur in most circumstances. | Quarterly |
| 3 | Possible | Might occur at some time; 50/50 chance. | Annually |
| 2 | Unlikely | Not expected; could occur in specific scenarios. | Every 2-3 years |
| 1 | Rare | Highly unlikely; only in exceptional cases. | Every 5+ years |

## 2. Impact Criteria (1-5)

| Level | Descriptor | Financial/Operational Impact | Data Privacy Impact |
| :---: | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 5 | Critical | >$1M loss; total system shutdown. | Massive PII/Export Control breach. |
| 4 | Major | $250k - $1M loss; significant downtime. | Significant unauthorized access. |
| 3 | Moderate | $50k - $250k loss; minor service disruption. | Isolated data leak; internal only. |
| 2 | Minor | <$50k loss; negligible operational impact. | Minimal risk to data integrity. |
| 1 | Insignificant | No financial loss; no downtime. | No data exposure. |

## 3. Risk Calculation & Heat Map
**Risk Score = Likelihood x Impact**

*   **1-8 (Low):** Acceptable risk; monitor periodically.
*   **9-15 (Medium):** Mitigation required; assign owner.
*   **16-25 (High):** Immediate action required; executive escalation.
